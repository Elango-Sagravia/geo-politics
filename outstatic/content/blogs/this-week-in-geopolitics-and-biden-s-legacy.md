Biden reversed Trump’s decision to take the US out of the WHO decision, seeking to restore global cooperation, especially in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.4">His most successful area was gaining trust and cooperation with America’s key allies. He handled the situation in Ukraine favorably and aligned with the EU and other NATO partners. Similarly, he did well with <a href="https://link.geopoliticalsummary.com/gs-18-01-2025-bo2-10-indo-pacific-allies">Indo-Pacific allies</a>, particularly Japan, South Korea, New Zealand, and Australia. Biden took a firm stance on China, viewing it as the most significant long-term geopolitical challenge for the US. He criticized its human rights record and made policies to lessen the US’s dependence on China. He also supported Taiwan while still advocating the One China policy. This is one of his foreign policy stances that aligns with most mainstream Democrats and Republicans.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.4">In some ways, his single term parallels the late Jimmy Carter’s single term. One comparison that can be made is the quick <a href="https://link.geopoliticalsummary.com/gs-18-01-2025-bo3-11-withdrawal-from-afghanistan">withdrawal from Afghanistan</a>, which led to the Taliban regaining control of the country and drew significant criticism for its execution and the abandonment of Afghan allies. However, Biden defended the decision, framing it as ending a "forever war." Many say this was a situation that he had little control over, and it was right that he did so. However, others said it brought to question US commitments and its reliability.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.4">An area where his legacy is less favorable is the situation between Israel and Palestine. In comparison, he was successful in being part of meditation in a ceasefire-hostage deal. However, it became very late, and it was debatable how much he had a hand in this. Many believe he could have <a href="https://link.geopoliticalsummary.com/gs-18-01-2025-bo4-12-pushed-the-israeli-government">pushed the Israeli government</a> harder; others say much of it was out of his control. Both the pro-Israeli and pro-Palestine camp claim he wasn’t as harsh on the other one.
