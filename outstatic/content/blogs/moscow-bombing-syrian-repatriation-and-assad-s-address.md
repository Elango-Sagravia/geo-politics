href="https://history.state.gov/milestones/1914-1920/paris-peace">Treaty of Versailles</a> was a peace document signed on June 28, 1919, to end World War I officially. The Paris Peace Conference convened in 1919 at <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/Palace+of+Versailles/@48.8048684,2.1177805,17z/data=!3m2!4b1!5s0x47e67dbeb2a2d1cb:0xfaa83490b67c0c36!4m6!3m5!1s0x47e67d94d7b14c75:0x538fcc15f59ce8f!8m2!3d48.8048649!4d2.1203554!16zL20vMGRfNjE?entry=ttu&amp;g_ep=EgoyMDI0MTIxMS4wIKXMDSoASAFQAw%3D%3D">Versailles</a> to determine peace terms. Germany had to accept full responsibility for the war. Germany lost territory, including areas like Alsace-Lorraine and its overseas colonies. Germany''s military was limited to 100,000 soldiers, and Germany was not allowed tanks, submarines, or an air force. Germany had to pay for war damages through reparations. Germany was not to be in the League of Nations, a precursor to the UN, a group meant to prevent future wars.</p><p style="font-size:16px;line-height:1.4">The treaty caused anger and <a href="https://www.history.com/news/treaty-of-versailles-world-war-ii-german-guilt-effects">resentment</a> in Germany, as many felt it was too harsh. Germany was not the party that initially started the First World War or the sole one responsible. However, along with Austria-Hungary, it was on the losing side. Historians argue it wasn’t fair that the defeated side didn’t get input in any of the conditions of the Paris Peace Treaty; even the more minor allied powers were given little to no say. Some believe that the long-term effects of this treaty contributed to the rise of Adolf Hitler and the start of <a href="https://www.britannica.com/event/World-War-II">World War
